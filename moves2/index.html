Hello there gamer,

If you are reading this chances are you've started playing Digimon World again, or made the mistake to listen to a friend who made it look like it wasn't going to be a huge part of your life...

...well to cut to the chase: This here is a javascript tool I originally wrote to merge two german movelists. The first was the only complete list of the german names, not much else to go around yet though. The second one contained the english names and which digimon can learn the moves.

By the time I had fixed some mistakes in the lists and merged them I had already stumbled across a third list containing comments about the attacks and how to get them, but more importantly an incomplete list of wild digimon with their active time and region.

So I merged that data into my tool, extending the digimon and tech objects I wrote, and just when I was done I came across the fourth list with a seemingly complete list of wild digimon and their techniques, as well as HP, Bits, Items and Comments.

When looking a bit more closely you will notice that there are some contradictory datasets in there as well as some doubles in the wild digimon section because it seemed better to have duplicates from the different lists than leaving some out.

I have also found a post about learning probabilities by romsstar which I will integrate once I've figured out what each Digimon's natures are or find a list that includes this information.

Some great information in german is available here there's also a link to a digivolution tool. I wrote a variation of that tool that allows you to see all possibilities from the egg onward. it can be found here.

A big thank you to romsstar for giving europeans the opportunity to play this game as it was originally intended, I still get excited when I think about the moment I found this and realised I could finally beat one of my all time favourite games.

Thanks also to all that contributed to the lists I integrated. Your work has kept a lot of gamers from losing their minds!

cheers
